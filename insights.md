# Project Insights & Findings

## 1. Customer Concentration & High-Value Buyers

The analysis showed that a relatively small percentage of customers contributed a significantly larger share of overall revenue. Using customer spending analysis, ranking functions, and customer lifetime value calculations, it became clear that high-value repeat customers played a major role in platform revenue generation.

This highlights the importance of customer retention strategies and loyalty-focused business models in e-commerce platforms.

---

## 2. Payment Method Preferences

Payment analysis revealed that certain payment methods dominated customer transactions across the platform. Credit-card-based transactions formed the largest share of total payments, indicating customer preference for installment and digital payment systems.

The project also analyzed:

* payment installments
* average payment value
* revenue contribution percentage by payment type

This provides insights into customer purchasing behavior and transaction flexibility preferences.

---

## 3. Revenue Distribution by Region

State-wise and city-wise revenue analysis showed that revenue generation was heavily concentrated in specific geographic regions. Certain states consistently generated higher order volumes and revenue compared to others.

This indicates:

* stronger customer demand in urban markets
* regional concentration of purchasing activity
* potential areas for business expansion and marketing optimization

---

## 4. Product Category Performance

Product category analysis identified the highest-performing product categories based on:

* revenue generation
* total products sold
* average pricing
* freight costs

Some categories generated high sales volume but lower average order values, while others generated fewer orders with significantly higher revenue per transaction.

This demonstrates how category-level analysis can help businesses optimize:

* pricing strategies
* inventory allocation
* product prioritization

---

## 5. Monthly Sales & Revenue Trends

Time-series analysis using monthly revenue trends showed fluctuations in purchasing activity across different months. Revenue aggregation and trend analysis indicated periods of stronger sales performance and seasonal purchasing patterns.

This type of analysis is important for:

* forecasting demand
* operational planning
* inventory management
* marketing campaign timing

---

## 6. Delivery & Operational Insights

CASE WHEN analysis was used to classify orders as:

* On-Time Deliveries
* Delayed Deliveries

This helped identify operational performance trends related to order fulfillment and estimated delivery timelines.

Such insights are valuable for improving:

* logistics efficiency
* customer satisfaction
* delivery planning

---

## 7. Freight Cost Analysis

Freight cost analysis across product categories revealed that certain categories consistently incurred higher logistics costs.

This can help businesses:

* optimize shipping strategies
* reduce operational costs
* improve profitability margins

---

## 8. Repeat Customer & Retention Analysis

Business KPI analysis showed that repeat customers contributed heavily toward sustained revenue generation. Customer order frequency and repeat purchase behavior indicated strong retention patterns within specific customer segments.

This type of analysis is commonly used in:

* CRM analytics
* retention marketing
* subscription business models
* customer loyalty optimization

---

## 9. Advanced SQL & Analytical Techniques Applied

This project also focused heavily on implementing advanced SQL concepts commonly used in real-world analytics environments.

The project included:

* INNER JOIN & LEFT JOIN operations
* Aggregate functions
* CASE WHEN logic
* Window Functions

  * RANK()
  * DENSE_RANK()
  * ROW_NUMBER()
* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* HAVING clauses
* Multi-table KPI analysis
* Revenue contribution calculations

These techniques helped simulate realistic business analysis workflows performed by data analysts and business intelligence professionals.

---

This project strengthened practical understanding of:

* relational databases
* schema relationships
* SQL-based analytics
* KPI generation
* business-focused problem solving
* real-world analytical workflows

It also improved understanding of how raw business data can be transformed into structured insights for decision-making and performance analysis.
